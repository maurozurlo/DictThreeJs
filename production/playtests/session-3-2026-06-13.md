# Playtest Session 3 — Hard Mode
**Date**: 2026-06-13
**Tester**: Mauro Zurlo (developer)
**Difficulty**: Hard ($150 starting treasury)
**Run outcome**: Lost — People overthrew the government (relations reached −10)

---

## Focus: Does Hard feel genuinely harder?

**Finding**: Yes. Even with aggressive tactics (lowering all budgets, maximizing taxes, expropriation) the game felt harder in a meaningful way. Hard mode creates real constraint without feeling arbitrary.

**Observed tactics**: Lower all expenditures + raise both tax rates + expropriate when possible. This worked for several rounds but eventually backfired on People relations.

---

## Focus: Round-3 cliff (grace period expiry)

**Finding**: Survivable. The transition from dampened to full relation penalties at round 3 is noticeable but not a cliff that ends runs instantly. System working as intended.

---

## Focus: Does grace period feel different in rounds 1–2?

**Finding**: Works well. Not obviously telegraphed (as designed), but round 1–2 give enough breathing room on Hard to not feel immediately impossible.

---

## Run outcome details

**Loss cause**: People relations reached −10. Overthrow triggered.
**Grammar bug found**: End-game text read "The people has overthrown your government!" — should be "have". Fixed post-session.

---

## Additional observations

**Periodic events**: Work well on Hard. No issues.
**Overall Hard verdict**: Genuine difficulty increase. Recommended to keep as-is.
