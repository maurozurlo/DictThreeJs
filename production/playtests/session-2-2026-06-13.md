# Playtest Session 2 — Mid-Game Systems
**Date**: 2026-06-13
**Tester**: Mauro Zurlo (developer)
**Difficulty**: Not specified
**Run outcome**: Reached rounds 5–8; observed recurring effects, relation management, budget tension

---

## Focus: Recurring effects legibility

**Finding**: Active laws and deals in the log are clear. Repeal function works and is intuitive. No issues.

---

## Focus: Relation management

**Finding**: Relations are predictable and manageable. Playing conservatively (dialogue, careful law choices) leads to safe but mediocre outcomes. Good design tension — safety has a cost.

---

## Focus: Budget tension

**Finding**: Budget pressure spikes at predictable moments rather than random ones. Periodic events (rounds 3, 6) are the main stress points. These land well and feel fair.

**Bug (kept as feature)**: Periodic event costs can push treasury negative. Player can agree to a $100M event with only $50M in treasury — game permits this, resulting in negative balance for the remainder of the round. Player must recover through budget adjustment or expropriation before advancing. Discovered to be good emergent gameplay.

---

## Additional observations

**Daily events**: Random per-round relation adjustments feel unnecessary by this stage of the game. They reduce clarity and player agency without adding interesting decisions. The game has sufficient complexity from periodic events, budget effects, laws, deals, and meet actions. Recommend removing.
